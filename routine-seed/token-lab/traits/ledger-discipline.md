# trait: LEDGER discipline — the lab notebook's spine

LEDGER.md is the append-only journal of this research program. It is how a run avoids
re-testing refuted methods and re-reading sources that gave nothing — **check it before
exploring anything new**.

- **Append one entry per run**, before finishing:
  `### <run id> — <one-line verdict>` followed by short bullets: what was measured (the
  baseline's headline delta), what was tested (method, arms, N), the verdict with effect
  size, backlog movements, and **methods rejected + why** (negative results are the
  fence against re-proposing — the most valuable lines in the file).
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "B saved 38% output tokens at equal grader score (N=4)"
  ages well; "B seemed better" is noise by next week.
- **Sources that were dry wells** get one line each — future runs must not re-read them.
- Keep entries under ~12 lines; the full detail lives in `state/experiments/` and the
  report. The LEDGER is the index of what happened, not the archive.
